How This Model Works

Three Categories of GPU Usage

Training runs are burst workloads — large parallel jobs that run for hours then stop. Design Lab reserved hours are dedicated to a single engineer during core business hours. Mirage reserved hours provide each developer their own GPU during working hours for interactive development.

Reserved vs. Surge

Reserved GPUs must be purchased — they're occupied during business hours by named owners and cannot be shared. Training campaigns surge onto these same GPUs during off-hours (evenings, nights, weekends), avoiding the need for additional hardware purchases.

When More GPUs Are Needed

If monthly training volume exceeds the reserved fleet's off-hours capacity, additional GPUs must be added to the fleet. The interactive model calculates this automatically. Off-hours capacity = fleet size × 16 hours/day × working days/month.

Reading the Summary

Reserved Fleet = minimum GPUs to purchase. Training Burst = how many GPUs run training concurrently during off-hours. Total GPUs to Purchase = reserved fleet + any additional GPUs needed if training overflows off-hours.
